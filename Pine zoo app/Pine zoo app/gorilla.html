GORILLA

Gorillas are the largest primates and are known for their strength and intelligence. They live in groups called troops, led by a dominant silverback Gorillas are primarily herbivores, feeding on leaves, stems, and fruit. They play a crucial role in their ecosystem by helping to disperse seeds. Gorillas are highly social animals, communicating through vocalizations, gestures, and facial expressions. They exhibit complex behaviors and emotions. Gorillas are critically endangered due to habitat loss and poaching. Conservation efforts are crucial to protect these magnificent creatures. Gorillas are found in the forests of central Africa, primarily in countries like Rwanda, Uganda, and the Democratic Republic of Congo. Gorillas are known for their strong family bonds and nurturing behavior, especially between mothers and their young. Gorillas have a diverse diet, which includes leaves, fruits, and flowers. They are known to consume up to 40 pounds of vegetation daily. Gorillas are capable of using tools, such as sticks to measure water depth or to extract termites from mounds.
